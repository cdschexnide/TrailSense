Analytics Redesign: Three Approaches

Same 4-tab structure (Overview, Signals, Devices, Patterns) — different content philosophy

A

Raw Data Forward

Surface the rich device telemetry directly — RSSI distributions, burst timing histograms, noise floor trends, confidence score breakdowns, proximity zone charts. Technical and detailed across all tabs. Think "Grafana for property security."

Pros

  • Maximizes the value of device data investment
  • Power users get full visibility into sensor performance
  • Unique differentiator — no competitor shows this level of RF detail

Cons

  • Overwhelming for non-technical users
  • Lots of charts that require RF knowledge to interpret
  • "What does burst interval variance mean for my security?"
B

Insight-Driven

Translate device data into security-meaningful insights — "3 new devices appeared this week", "Activity spiked 40% on Tuesday nights", "Device w_a3f has visited 12 times, always between 2-4 AM." Raw metrics hidden, conclusions surfaced.

Pros

  • Immediately actionable — tells you what matters
  • Accessible to any property owner, not just RF engineers
  • Feels like a smart security product, not a spectrum analyzer

Cons

  • Wastes the rich data — users can't explore or verify
  • Hides the "why" behind conclusions
  • Less differentiated — any app can show summary cards
C

Layered: Insights Up Top, Data Underneath

Overview tab is insight-driven — key metrics, anomalies, period comparison, "what changed." Signals/Devices/Patterns tabs go deep — RSSI distributions, proximity breakdowns, device behavior timelines, temporal heatmaps. The Overview tells you what to care about; the other tabs let you explore why.

Pros

  • Casual users get value from Overview without going deeper
  • Power users can drill into the full signal data
  • Overview insights can link directly to the relevant deep tab
  • Best of both worlds — accessible AND technically rich

Cons

  • Most to design and implement (4 distinct tab experiences)
  • Needs thoughtful information hierarchy to avoid redundancy between Overview and detail tabs

⟶ Recommended. Matches how the app already works — Home screen gives you the quick picture, Alerts/Radar/Devices let you go deep. Same principle applied to analytics.
